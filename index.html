Bottom line up front

Four conclusions for a 30-second read

Priority 0 2026 is a margin recession, not a creative mystery.

Every variant that ran in both years earned dramatically less per close in 2026 (J521: $832→$350 · J568: $775→$274 · J655: +$808→−$757). Account-wide margin per close fell $542→$89. Decomposed: ~60% is CPA inflation (CPL $113→$127, close rate flat), ~40% is lower enrolled debt per close (~$24.2k→~$22.5k). Same ads, worse economics — that points at the environment (lead costs + who's in market), not at the creative recipe. T2

Priority 1 The engine recipe is confirmed — and it isn't J521 bias.

On the full-year signal cohort (51 ads ≥5 closes, 85% of spend): CPA is still the #1 MPU driver (−0.53; −0.68 at ≥10 closes), nearly unchanged with J521 removed. At the creative layer the same recipe carries both years: Before-After hooks (+$730/close on 787 closes) · peer-creator voice (+$689 on 928) · outcome/"simplify & protect" framing (+$728/close ex-J521). The lever is who the ad recruits and what it promises — close-rate execution itself sits with Freedom's sales floor.

Revision Four June conclusions failed replication. Don't brief them.

The debt anchor is not the targeting (anchor $ ↔ FICO −0.14 in 2025 vs +0.60 claimed; anchor ↔ MPU −0.04). Cheap CPL is not a trap (sub-$110 ads closed 6.7% vs 6.2% and made 2.2× the margin in 2025). Statics aren't net losers (+$104k in 2025, just weaker per close). No utilization sweet spot (margin flat across 67–78% bands). T1

Methodology, confidence & limitations

How this was built — and what would raise confidence

Sources & grain. 2025: Sam's authoritative per-variant full-year passback ("2025 YTD Performance By Creative Total", 616 variants, $3.78M) merged with Snowflake asset URLs; MPU is Sam's pre-computed margin per enrollment and reproduces as ≈ 9.7% × ABI − CPA (r = 0.99). CPA = cost per close (~$1,800), not CPL (~$113). 2026 YTD: the June dash dataset (215 assets, $1.93M, Launches-freedom-financial-2026-06-02). All 2026 comparisons use that dash's published aggregates.

Classification. 130 of 616 ads (every ad ≥$1k spend with a recoverable asset, 83% of 2025 spend) were re-watched by the 5-stage Gemini + Claude classifier (hook, voice, motivator, formula, format, transcript; "moderate" reliability). Debt anchors were extracted from transcripts by pattern-match — crude but consistent across both years' methods.

Aggregation & floors. All cohort metrics are close-weighted (total margin ÷ closes), the correct aggregate for a per-enrollment number. Correlations use ads ≥5 closes (n=51, 85% of spend); attribute cohorts use ≥3 closes with a ≥20-close floor per group; every figure carries n and closes. Ex-J521V1B values shown or checked throughout (Brandon's bias concern) — no headline finding depends on it.

Comparability caveats. 2026 is ~5 months in a compressed-margin regime — cross-year levels aren't comparable, directions are. Profile fields (FICO/HHI/utilization) are modeled/appended, ad-grain, and should be arbitrated at adset grain (Sam's rule). Age-delivery data exists only from Feb 2026; using it as a proxy for 2025 creatives assumes a creative's age-pull is stable (survivor-biased panel, n=11–15). 264 sub-$1k-spend ads (3% of spend, ~0–2 closes each) were left unclassified as statistical noise; 2025 asset files were unrecoverable for 43% of variants (15% of spend) — mostly older, low-spend ads.

Confidence. T1 descriptive · T2 inferential (confounders named) · T3 hypothesis to test. The replication scoreboard is T1 (it reports what each panel shows); the recruit-two-sided read and the quiz-expectation mechanism are T2; the age-proxy panel is T3.

Would raise confidence next. Adset-grain re-aggregation with Sam · a 2024 panel (does the recipe replicate three-for-three?) · an isolated quiz-anchor test · hand-coded anchors on the top 50.
