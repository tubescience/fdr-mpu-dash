MPU is a conversion problem
disguised as a targeting problem.

The #revenue-freedom analysis concluded the on-screen debt anchor "IS the targeting." The full dataset says the number a creative shows moves the lead's credit profile — but not its margin. What moves MPU is CPA, and CPA is CPL ÷ close-rate. The lever is lead→enrollment conversion, not the dollar on screen.

MPU ≈ 0.10 × ABI − CPA (r = 0.945) CPA↔MPU −0.72 · close-rate↔MPU +0.62 · ABI↔MPU −0.003 Net YTD margin +$82k · J521V1B = 71% of it
Bottom line up front

Three conclusions a busy reader can take in 30 seconds

Priority 0 The account is one ad deep.

Net YTD margin is +$82k and J521V1B alone is +$58k (≈71%). Defending and cloning that one asset before it fatigues is more urgent than any optimization. The flashy high-MPU ads (J737 +5,786, J679A +4,348) are 2–4-close noise.

Priority 1 Build for close rate, not the anchor.

On the signal cohort, MPU tracks CPA (−0.72) and close rate (+0.62); ABI/debt size is flat (−0.003). The #1 ad shows no debt figure; a textbook "prime profile" ad (FICO 623) lost $11k on a 4% close. The recipe that wins is structural, not numeric.

Priority 1 The winning recipe is a conversion recipe.

MoF · Before-After hook · outcome / "simplify & protect" frame · authentic peer or real-graduate voice · video. Curiosity-Gap and Testimonial-Lead hooks, sustained crisis/shame framing, and text statics are MPU-negative.

Priority 2 Stop chasing cheap CPL.

Cheap leads close worse (4.7% vs 7.6%) and the low-CPL cohort lost $8k while pricier leads made +$113k. CPL is uncorrelated with margin (r = −0.02). The Q1 "drive CPL down / scale statics" instinct was margin-negative.

Methodology, confidence & limitations

How this was built — and what would raise confidence

Source & grain. Launches-freedom-financial-2026-06-02_classified.xlsx, YTD, ad-variant level (215 unique assets) merged with Sam's MPU/profile passback. MPU = margin per enrollment ≈ 0.10 × ABI − CPA (validated, r = 0.945; reproduces the Feb #revenue-freedom zone reports). CPA here is cost-per-close (~$2,000), not CPL (~$130).

Cohorts. Only 19 ads have ≥10 closes, 27 ≥5, 39 ≥3; 145 have zero. Correlations use closes ≥5 (85% of spend); creative-attribute breakdowns use closes ≥3 with a ≥6-close floor per group. All metrics are close-weighted (total margin ÷ closes) — the correct aggregate for a per-enrollment number.

Confidence. T1 descriptive (the data shows X) · T2 inferential (confounders named) · T3 speculative (a hypothesis to test). The anchor finding is T2 (crude regex anchor extraction, small n); the "outcome beats problem" read is T3 (rests on J521).

Two caveats before betting the account on this. (1) This is ad-level data — Sam's rule is to judge at the adset level; re-run there and expect the noise outliers to wash out while the structural signals hold. (2) The FICO/HHI/utilization fields are ungraded in the file's reliability sheet and likely modeled/appended, not hard credit pulls — treat profile precision as directional. All creative tags (motivator, funnel, hook, voice, format) are Gemini-classified at "Moderate" reliability.

Not in scope. Adset-level re-aggregation, hand-coded anchors, a date/fatigue dimension, and isolating the call-center close-rate contribution from the creative's — each would sharpen this and is recommended as follow-up.
