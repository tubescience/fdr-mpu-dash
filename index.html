Margin per enrollment fell 84%.
Here's what the data says about bringing it back.

MPU — Freedom's margin per enrollment, now in our data passbacks — collapsed from $542/close across full-year 2025 to $89/close in 2026 YTD. To find the levers, we re-watched and AI-classified every classifiable 2025 ad (130 ads, 83% of spend), tied every creative attribute to margin, decomposed the decline through the media funnel, and audited the account's current media setup. The short version: the decline is mostly priced-in economics (lead costs +26% CPM, lighter debt loads), not a creative failure — but there are three stat-significant creative levers and a set of media-side ideas that can claw margin back.

Bottom line up front

Four conclusions for a 30-second read

What happened The decline is mostly economics, not creative.

The same ads earn far less per close in 2026 than they did in 2025 (J521V1B: $832 → $350/close; every shared ad fell). Decomposed: ~60% is acquisition-cost inflation — CPM is up 26% — and ~40% is lighter enrolled debt per close (~$24.2k → ~$22.5k), with close rates flat. When identical creative produces worse margins, the cause sits in the auction and the lead pool, not the ads. T2

Creative levers Three creative factors move MPU at statistical significance.

Across 51 ads with real signal (≥5 closes, 85% of spend): outcome/relief framing beats problem/pain framing (+$279/close, p=0.001) · straight product offers beat quiz/calculator-led ads (+$544/close, p=0.003) · Before-After story hooks beat testimonial-style openers (+$559/close, p=0.015). All hold with the #1 ad removed. These are the brief-able levers.

Myth check The popular levers don't actually move margin.

Things that look like levers but show no relationship with MPU in the full-year data: the dollar figure an ad shows on screen (corr with MPU −0.04), cheap vs expensive leads (cheap-CPL ads actually made 2.2× the margin), FICO-prime audiences (higher-FICO pools closed worse), and card-utilization bands (margin flat from 67–78%). Briefing against these spends effort where there's no payoff. T1

Reading rule One ad's MPU is a coin flip. Judge families and cohorts.

Sibling variants of the same concept differ by a median $1,692/close — twice the portfolio's spread. J568V1D made +$775/close while its sibling J568V1C lost $2,171. No MPU verdict below ~20 closes; concepts get judged at the family level, audiences at the ad-set level.

The mechanics

What MPU is, and what actually moves it

MPU is Freedom's margin per enrollment: roughly 9.7% of the debt a customer enrolls (ABI), minus what it cost to close them (CPA) — the formula reproduces Sam's passback numbers at r = 0.99. So only two things can move it: recruit people carrying more enrollable debt, or lower the cost of a close. Everything below hangs off those two terms.

Correlation of each factor with MPU — 51 ads with ≥5 closes, 85% of 2025 spend

Removing the #1 ad (J521V1B) changes every bar by ≤0.02 — none of this is one ad's gravity. T1

Both terms matter. CPA is the strongest single factor (−0.53, strengthening to −0.68 among ads with ≥10 closes), and enrolled debt is right behind it (+0.51). CPA itself is driven by close rate (corr −0.61), not lead price (CPL ↔ close rate ≈ 0) — a close gets cheap when the right person walks in, not when the click was cheap.

🔍
Who actually closes (it's not who you'd guess)

Close rate is Freedom's sales floor working whatever leads creative sends — we don't dial it directly. But we can choose who walks in, and the data flips the usual instinct: higher-FICO lead pools closed worse (−0.36), higher-utilization pools closed better (+0.40). People with options — balance transfers, consolidation loans — take them; people genuinely stuck enroll. The profitable recruit is two-sided: carrying real, large debt (drives the margin ceiling) and genuinely stuck-but-solvent (drives the close) — not maximally "prime."     Messaging frame

    The strongest lever in the account (p=0.001) — and the most under-built: 3 outcome-framed concepts vs 48 problem-framed. The winning register is confident relief — "turn 3 payments into 1, protect your savings" — not pain and rescue.

    Offer mechanic

    Quiz/calculator-led ads earn a fraction of straight offers (p=0.003) and their leads close 25% worse (5.1% vs 6.8%). Working hypothesis: the calculator sets a "quick savings" expectation the sales call has to unwind.

    Hook archetype

    Before-After story openers are #1 (787 closes); testimonial-style openers are last (p=0.015 against Before-After).

    Voice

    A relatable peer creator telling their own story carries the portfolio (928 closes). Actor reads are the weak lane (gap p=0.074 — directional).

    Script structure

    Outcome-led stories (OS) dominate at scale — 808 closes at +$716.

    Video vs static

    Video earns more per close but the gap is NOT statistically solid (p=0.22) — and statics netted +$104k in 2025. Statics are a legitimate cheap-reach lane, not a banned one.

    The margin recipe, in one line: a relatable peer creator (or clean text/GFX), opening on a Before-After story, framed as confident relief — "simplify & protect," not crisis — with a straight offer and a sound-off-legible benefit banner. The losers to retire: testimonial-style openers, straight actor reads, and calculator-gated offers.

    The most tempting non-lever

    The on-screen debt number doesn't pick your audience

    A natural theory — one we took seriously — is that the dollar figure an ad leads with ("I had $42,000 in credit card debt") screens its own audience: bigger number, more qualified viewer, more margin. We extracted the debt figure from all 34 signal ads that show one. It correlates with nothing — not the credit profile of the leads, not their debt, not margin. T1

    Margin by anchor band

    The "none" group is inflated by J521V1B (which shows no number at all); without it, anchored ads actually edge ahead (+$521 vs +$258, p=0.056 — directional).

    Practical read: use whatever dollar figure serves the story. Don't raise calculator defaults, rewrite qualifiers, or kill concepts over the number on screen expecting margin to move — the #1 ad in the account shows no number at all, and high-anchor ads (934 closes) perform fine.

    If the pressure is the ecosystem — ideas worth testing

    Recovery ideas beyond the brief

    The decline is mostly priced-in economics, so creative alone won't fully reverse it. These are test ideas that point delivery at where margin demonstrably lives — all T3 (hypotheses with supporting data, not proven findings), and all joint conversations with Freedom's media team since we don't run this account's media.

    Media · test Express the 45+ skew in delivery, not just casting

    Margin concentrates in older delivery: among shared variants, the profitable ads delivered 58–79% of spend to 45+, while the two worst margin-losers delivered youngest (50–53%) — yet every live ad set targets 18–65 broad with Advantage+ free to expand toward cheap young inventory. Worth a test cell: duplicate the proven recipe into a 35+/45+ ad-set split (Meta retired manual bid multipliers; age-min cells are the available instrument) and read backend MPU per cell. Caveat: age-pull didn't predict margin across the full 2025 panel (corr ≈ 0) — this is a guardrail against the cheap-young drift the funnel shows, not a silver bullet.

    Owner: Freedom media + TS (we supply the creative pairs). 45+ data: Meta demo splits since Feb 2026.
    Media · ask Check whether the LQS optimization event sees debt size

    The account already bids against a lead-quality score — smart. But MPU's second term is enrolled debt (+0.51 with margin), and if LQS weights "likely to submit" more than "carrying $20k+," the algorithm is optimizing to the wrong half of the formula while debt-per-close slides (−7% YoY). The ask: how is LQS built, and can estimated debt load (or predicted ABI) be weighted in — or a value-based event tested against it?

    Methodology, confidence & limitations

    How this was built — and what would raise confidence

    Sources & grain. 2025: Sam's per-variant full-year passback ("2025 YTD Performance By Creative Total", 616 variants, $3.78M TS spend) — MPU is Sam's pre-computed margin per enrollment, reproducing as ≈ 9.7% × ABI − CPA (r = 0.99). CPA = cost per close (~$1,800), not cost per lead (~$113). 2026 YTD: the June classified dataset (215 assets, $1.93M). Meta-side funnel and setup: Snowflake (whole account, both years); note Meta and the backend count leads differently — cross-system gaps are read directionally.

    Classification. 130 of 616 ads — every 2025 ad ≥$1k spend with a recoverable asset file, 83% of spend — re-watched by a 5-stage Gemini + Claude classifier (hook, voice, framing, structure, offer, transcript; moderate reliability). On-screen debt figures extracted from transcripts by pattern match.

    Aggregation & floors. Cohort metrics are close-weighted (total margin ÷ closes) — the correct aggregate for a per-enrollment number. Correlations use ads ≥5 closes (n=51, 85% of spend); attribute groups use ≥3 closes with a ≥20-close floor; every figure carries its n. Significance is a 4,000-draw bootstrap at the ad level. Every chart shows an "excl. top ad" bar — no finding leans on J521V1B.

    Limitations. Lead profile fields (FICO/income/utilization) are modeled and ad-grain — validate at ad-set grain. Age-delivery data exists only from Feb 2026 (used as a proxy for each creative's age-pull; survivor-biased, n=11–15). 264 sub-$1k ads (3% of spend) were left unclassified; asset files were unrecoverable for 43% of 2025 variants (15% of spend), mostly older low-spend ads. Placement-mix history before March 2026 isn't available to us.

    Confidence. T1 descriptive · T2 inferential (confounders named) · T3 hypothesis to test. The three creative levers are bootstrap-significant T1/T2; the recruit-profile read and lead-dilution story are T2; the delivery-skew and LQS ideas are T3 tests.

    Would raise confidence next. Ad-set-grain validation with Sam · a 2024 panel (three-year replication) · the LQS construction answer · an isolated 35+/45+ delivery test · hand-coded anchors on the top 50.
